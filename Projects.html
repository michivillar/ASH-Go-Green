⋆*･ﾟ:⋆*･ﾟ Recycling Station Designs ⋆*･ﾟ:⋆*･ﾟ

Although there are recycling bins in classrooms, the halls only have general garbage bins. This creates an issue, as people are forced to use the garbage bins during lunch and between periods. Specifically during lunch, lots of waste is produced. Inspired by the designs found in malls such as Bayshore, the woodworking and manufacturing classes have been working on building and designing a recycling station for the Atrium, which is a bin with three holes, for each type of waste. In the future, the Go Green will raise money to make similar stations on each floor.

⋆⋅☆⋅⋆ Eco Schools Designation ⋆⋅☆⋅⋆

EcoSchools Canada is a charity that offers a certification program for elementary through secondary schools. Each year, schools apply for certification by undertaking environmental actions, campaigns, and projects, and then recording them. At the end of the year, schools recieve a certification based on how eco-friendly they were. Last year we attained a Gold status. We're going for Platinum this year.

˗ˏˋ ♡ Goulbourn Forced Road - Bicycle lane initiative ♡ ˎˊ˗

Last year, students created a petition to build a bike lane on Golbourn Forced Road which amassed 115 signatures. They presented this to councillor Cathy Curry who explained that a new road will be constructed with a bike lane once the drainage system is approved by the NCC. The proposal was sent out over 15 years ago, so instead of waiting for even longer, students have decided to express their interest in the approval of this drainage system so that they can get their bike lane as quickly as possible! Additionally, this drainage system being approved is very important to the preservation of Trillium Woods (where the Beaver Pond is and behind Richcraft), because without it they'll take down the forest and an artificial pond will be made.
